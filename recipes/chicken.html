Sous Vide Chicken

Sous vide chicken is a great way to meal prep your protein for the week or two. Whether you pan-fry them with some oil or chop them cold for a salad, there are many ways to use chicken breast made this way. Sous vide helps retain a lot of the juicy tenderness while also being a lot more forgiving if overcooked.

Ingredients

  • 4-5 lb boneless chicken breast
  • 4-5 tsp salt
  • 4-5 tsp black pepper

Instructions

  1. Set sous vide to 150 F.
  2. In a large bowl, add your chicken breast, salt and pepper, and any other seasoning you may want to include. Mix throughly to cover all parts of the meat.
  3. Place individual breast into vacuum bags and vacuum seal shut, or alternatively, you can use ziploc bags pulling as much air as you can out.
  4. Place bags into water bath for at least 1 hour and 30 minutes to ensure that they are cooked through.
  5. Once cooked, you can take them out of the bag, pat dry, and pan fry each side, for a couple of minutes each side, for a nice golden crust or cool them down via an ice bath to store in the refrigerater/freezer for a later date.
